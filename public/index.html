Predict curb-choke gridlocks
before they happen.

RouteSync uses XGBoost trained on 298,000+ Bengaluru traffic violations to detect illegal-parking-induced bottlenecks and trigger automated pickup-point geofencing for ride-hailing apps.

How it works
Three-stage anomaly detection pipeline
1

Historical Clustering

K-Means extracts 20 high-density parking violation centroids from the dataset — these become the city's structural choke-risk zones.

train_model.py · K-Means
2

ML Classification

Live telemetry (speed, delay, upstream flow) is fed to an XGBoost model. It classifies whether the slowdown is a standard queue or an active curb blockage.

app.py · XGBoost
3

Geofence Dispatch

When a choke is confirmed, the orchestrator pushes a pin-shift payload to ride-hailing aggregators — relocating pickups away from the blocked curb.

server.js · Webhooks
